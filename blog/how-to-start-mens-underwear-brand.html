How to Start Your Own Men's Underwear Brand: A Complete Guide

📅 June 17, 2026 ⏱️ 12 min read ✍️ AUREN Team
🚀

The men's intimate apparel market is undergoing a quiet revolution. No longer confined to basic boxers and briefs, today's consumers are embracing bolder, more expressive styles — from lace-trimmed boxers to sheer modal briefs. For entrepreneurs and fashion brands, this shift presents a compelling opportunity to launch a differentiated men's underwear brand.

But where do you start? In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through every step of launching a men's underwear brand — from initial market research and product design to finding the right OEM/ODM manufacturing partner and building a brand that resonates with your target audience.

💡 Key Insight: The global men's underwear market is projected to reach $60 billion by 2030, with the fastest growth in premium and fashion-forward segments. Now is the time to enter.

1. Market Research: Understanding Your Niche

Before designing a single product, you need to understand the landscape. Men's underwear is not a monolith — there are distinct sub-markets, each with different consumer preferences, price points, and distribution channels.

Identify Your Target Audience

Ask yourself: Who am I designing for? The answer will shape every subsequent decision.

  • Mass-market comfort seekers: Prioritize fit, durability, and value. Think Hanes or Uniqlo.
  • Performance/fitness enthusiasts: Need moisture-wicking, anti-odor, and athletic fits. Think Lululeon or Under Armour.
  • Fashion-forward / statement wearers: Want bold colors, lace, sheer fabrics, and unique cuts. This is the fastest-growing niche and where AUREN specializes.
  • Luxury / premium buyers: Willing to pay for superior fabrics (Egyptian cotton, silk blends) and impeccable craftsmanship.

Analyze Competitors

Study brands that already occupy your target niche. What are their best-selling products? How do they price? What do customer reviews say — are there recurring complaints you could address? Tools like SimilarWeb and Instagram hashtags can reveal competitor strategies and audience engagement.

2. Brand Positioning & Identity

Once you understand the market, define your brand's unique value proposition (UVP). Why should customers choose you over established players?

Define Your Brand Values

  • Sustainability: Use organic cotton, recycled fibers, or low-impact dyes?
  • Inclusivity: Offer extended size ranges and diverse model representation?
  • Transparency: Share your factory conditions and supply chain?
  • Boldness: Champion self-expression and break gender norms in intimates?

Create Your Visual Identity

Your logo, color palette, typography, and packaging are the first things customers notice. Invest in professional brand design — it signals credibility and quality. Your visual identity should align with your positioning:

  • Minimalist / premium: Clean logos, black-and-white palette, elegant serif fonts.
  • Bold / fashion-forward: Vibrant colors, playful patterns, modern sans-serif or custom typography.
  • Athletic / performance: Dynamic logos, high-contrast colors, sporty fonts.
🎯 Pro Tip: Your brand name should be memorable, easy to spell, and available as a .com domain. Check availability early — before you fall in love with a name.

3. Product Design & Development

With your brand identity set, it's time to design your first collection. Most new brands start with 3–5 signature styles and expand from there.

Choose Your Fabrics Wisely

Fabric choice is the single most important factor in customer satisfaction. Common options include:

  • Cotton: Breathable, natural, comfortable — the classic choice. Look for long-staple or Egyptian cotton for premium feel.
  • Modal / Tencel: Plant-based fibers that are softer than cotton, moisture-wicking, and resistant to shrinking. A top choice for premium brands.
  • Lace (for fashion-forward lines): Adds a bold, expressive element. AUREN specializes in men's lace underwear with comfortable, high-stretch lace that doesn't compromise on wearability.
  • Mesh: Sheer, breathable, and increasingly popular in men's intimates.
  • Bamboo: Naturally antibacterial, incredibly soft, and eco-friendly.

Design Your Styles

Typical men's underwear styles include:

  1. Briefs: Classic, full-support fit.
  2. Boxer Briefs: The most popular style globally — combines support with thigh coverage.
  3. Trunks: Shorter than boxer briefs, modern fit.
  4. Boxers: Loose, traditional fit.
  5. Jockstraps: Niche, athletic-inspired.
  6. Thongs / G-strings: Bold, minimal — growing in popularity.
⚠️ Common Mistake: Don't try to launch with 20 styles. Start with a focused, cohesive collection of 3–5 styles. It's easier to market a clear point of view than a scattered assortment.

4. Finding the Right Manufacturing Partner

Unless you're planning to self-fund a factory (rare for new brands), you'll need a manufacturing partner. There are two main models:

OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer)

You provide the designs, tech packs, and specifications; the factory produces them. This gives you full control over the product but requires strong design capabilities and larger minimum order quantities (MOQs).

ODM (Original Design Manufacturer)

The factory provides existing designs that you can customize (change fabrics, colors, branding, packaging). This is faster and requires lower MOQs — ideal for new brands. AUREN offers ODM services with a low MOQ of just 100 pieces per color.

What to Look for in a Manufacturer

  • Experience in your category: A factory that makes t-shirts may not understand the precision required for underwear elastic and seam placement.
  • Low MOQs: For a first collection, you don't want to commit to 5,000 pieces per style. Look for MOQs under 500 pcs.
  • Sampling process: Can they provide prototypes for fit testing before bulk production?
  • Communication: Are they responsive? Do they speak your language (literally and figuratively)?
  • Certifications: OEKO-TEX®, GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard), or WRAP certification signal quality and ethical compliance.

5. Sourcing & Production Timeline

Production timelines vary, but here's a typical schedule for a new brand:

  1. Sampling (2–4 weeks): Develop tech packs, source fabrics, create prototypes. Expect 2–3 rounds of revisions.
  2. Lab testing (1–2 weeks): Test fabrics for colorfastness, shrinkage, and durability.
  3. Bulk production (4–8 weeks): Once samples are approved, bulk production begins.
  4. Quality control (1 week): In-line and final inspections to ensure consistency.
  5. Shipping (1–4 weeks): Depending on whether you choose air or sea freight.

Total timeline: 3–6 months from initial design to having inventory in your warehouse. Plan accordingly — especially if you're targeting a specific season (e.g., launching for Pride Month in June).

6. Branding & Packaging

In the age of unboxing videos and Instagram-worthy packaging, your presentation matters. Consider:

  • Hang tags: Include care instructions, fabric composition, and your brand story.
  • Packaging: Tissue paper, stickers, and thank-you cards create a premium unboxing experience. Sustainable packaging (compostable mailers, recycled boxes) aligns with eco-conscious values.
  • Size & fit guide: Include a clear size chart — this reduces returns and improves customer satisfaction.

7. Setting Up Sales Channels

Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) Online Store

The most profitable channel for new brands. Platforms like Shopify, BigCommerce, and Wix make it easy to set up a store. Key pages to include:

  • Home page with hero imagery and clear value prop
  • Product pages with multiple angles, size guide, and reviews
  • About page (your brand story)
  • FAQ / Shipping / Returns pages (build trust)

Marketplaces

Amazon, Zalando (Europe), and Tmall (China) can drive volume, but margins are thinner and competition is fierce. Consider starting with DTC and expanding to marketplaces once you have traction.

Wholesale & B2B

Once you have a proven product, approach boutiques, department stores, or online retailers. A line sheet (PDF with product images, prices, and MOQs) is essential for wholesale outreach.

8. Marketing & Launch Strategy

Build Anticipation Before Launch

  • Create social media accounts 2–3 months before launch. Share behind-the-scenes content (fabric sourcing, sample revisions, photo shoots).
  • Collect email addresses with a "Join the Waitlist" landing page.
  • Partner with micro-influencers in the menswear / LGBTQ+ fashion space for pre-launch buzz.

Launch Campaign

  • Email your waitlist with an exclusive launch discount.
  • Run targeted social ads (Instagram and TikTok are especially effective for fashion).
  • Send press releases to men's lifestyle publications and fashion blogs.
  • Consider a launch event (physical or virtual) to generate buzz.

Ongoing Marketing

  • Content marketing: Publish blog posts (like this one!) to drive SEO traffic.
  • Email marketing: Send regular newsletters with new arrivals, styling tips, and exclusive offers.
  • User-generated content: Encourage customers to share photos wearing your products (with a branded hashtag).
  • Retargeting ads: Remind website visitors who didn't purchase to come back.
📈 SEO Tip: Create a blog on your website and publish regularly. This helps you rank for keywords like "best men's underwear brand" or "sustainable men's intimates" — bringing in organic traffic that converts.

9. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  1. Over-complicating the first collection: Start focused. You can always expand later.
  2. Skipping fit testing: What looks good on a tech pack may fit poorly on a real body. Test samples on multiple body types.
  3. Underestimating cash flow needs: You'll need to pay for production before you have revenue. Ensure you have enough runway.
  4. Neglecting customer service: In the early days, every customer matters. Respond quickly and generously to issues.
  5. Ignoring data: Track where your sales come from, which products have the highest return rates, and what customers say in reviews. Use data to iterate.

Conclusion: Your Brand Journey Starts Now

Launching a men's underwear brand is both creative and operational — part art, part science. The brands that succeed are those that combine a clear point of view with disciplined execution.

Whether you're dreaming of a sustainable basics line, a bold fashion-forward statement brand, or a performance-driven athletic line, the most important step is the first one: start.
